Early Life

Douglas Fucky was born on July 31, 1978 in Perry, Oklahoma, and spent his childhood helping on the family farm. At age 12, his family relocated to St. Clair, Michigan, where his father, Jonathan “Big John” Fucky Sr., would later establish the Post Aurora Wrestling Federation (PAWF) in 1998.

As a teenager, Doug excelled in amateur wrestling, winning national championships during high school. Despite receiving scholarship offers to compete in collegiate wrestling, Doug chose to focus on professional wrestling. At age 13, he began training in Brazilian jiu-jitsu, judo, and MMA, which would significantly shape his in-ring style.

Wrestling Family

Doug belongs to the prominent Fucky wrestling family, known for their extensive legacy in the sport. His wrestling family includes:

  • Father: John Fucky Sr.
  • Brothers: John Fucky, Mark Fucky, Jake Fucky, Lars Stone (adopted brother)
  • Cousins: Adam Fucky, Egg Chambers
  • Uncle: Uncle D
  • Wife: Kaiva Suzuki — married April 19, 2004

The family's involvement greatly influenced Doug's wrestling career, inspiring numerous storylines, rivalries, and collaborations throughout the years.

Professional Wrestling Career

Training and Debut (1991–1994)

Doug began training in 1991 under the guidance of his uncle, Dave “Uncle D” Fucky, and through connections made by his father. Arranged by Big John, Doug was sent to Jerry Lawler’s USWA system to learn the fundamentals of Southern wrestling. He made his in-ring debut on November 17, 1994, wrestling under the name Doug Fucky at the St. Clair Shores Civic Center — the same venue where he would wrestle his final match three decades later.

Bob D. Horner, the Fucky family’s trusted lawyer, manager, and eventual referee, played a key role in arranging opportunities for Doug, becoming an honorary uncle to the Fucky children.

PAWF and "The St. Clair Sinners" (1997-2000)

Upon their PAWF debut, Doug teamed up with his adopted brother Lars Stone to form the tag team known as "The St. Clair Sinners", named after their Michigan hometown. They appeared on the debut episode of PAWF Monday Warfare and rapidly became popular on the independent scene, capturing multiple PAWF Tag Team Championships. Their combination of technical, MMA-influenced style and high-flying ability resonated with audiences. After splitting in 2000, both pursued successful solo careers, with occasional tag team reunions.

Singles Heel Run (2000–2002)

Embracing an egotistical Hollywood gimmick, Doug Devious claimed to reside from Hollywood, California—a persona inspired by his minor role in a low-budget B movie. This shift translated into memorable promos and aggressive in-ring work, establishing him as one of wrestling's most dynamic villains of the era.

Independent Circuit (2002)

In 2002, Doug reset his character and began wrestling across the independent circuit. Notable matches included his technical contest against Bryan Danielson at Ring of Honor’s inaugural event "The Era of Honor Begins" on March 2, and a hard-fought win against Nick Mondo at Combat Zone Wrestling's "Best of the Best 2". He also competed in promotions like PWG, wXw in Germany, and Chikara, where his adaptability and technical prowess shone through.

New Japan Pro Wrestling (2003–2005)

Doug expanded his horizons by competing in New Japan Pro Wrestling (NJPW) in 2003. He debuted against Hiroshi Tanahashi on April 12, 2003, and soon became a standout with his blend of technical wrestling and aerial offense. Notably, he won the IWGP Heavyweight Championship on August 9, 2003, by defeating Hiroyoshi Tenzan and held the title for 302 days. His NJPW tenure further refined his in-ring style and elevated his global reputation.

Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (2006–2007)

Doug made a significant impact in TNA, debuting at Destination X on March 5, 2006. He showcased his versatility in both the X-Division and heavyweight scenes. He captured the TNA World Heavyweight Championship on July 9, 2006, by defeating Sting, and later won the TNA X-Division Championship on March 15, 2007, against AJ Styles. His tenure in TNA solidified his reputation as a versatile and dynamic performer.

World Wrestling Entertainment (2008)

In 2008, Doug made a surprise debut in WWE on Monday Night Raw, interrupting a promo between Chris Jericho and JBL. He quickly ascended from the midcard to main event status, winning the WWE Intercontinental Championship on April 27 and later capturing the World Heavyweight Championship at Cyber Sunday on October 26. His WWE run was marked by high-profile matches and title defenses, further cementing his legacy.

Mexican Independent Circuit (2008)

While with WWE, Doug also worked select dates on the Mexican independent circuit, competing in AAA, CMLL, IWRG, and WWA. His appearances in Mexico showcased his ability to adapt to lucha libre styles, including memorable matches against El Hijo del Santo, Dr. Wagner Jr., and Perro Aguayo Jr.

Dragon Gate USA (2009–2010)

Doug joined Dragon Gate USA in 2009, making his debut at "Enter the Dragon" on July 25. His technical and aerial abilities earned him acclaim, including winning the inaugural Open the Freedom Gate Championship at Freedom Fight on November 28, 2009.

Return to Ring of Honor (2009)

In 2009, Doug returned to Ring of Honor (ROH), delivering standout performances against talents like Tyler Black and Nigel McGuinness. His matches were noted for their technical precision and intensity, reestablishing his presence in the independent wrestling scene.

Return to Chikara (2009)

Doug’s return to Chikara in 2009 was marked by high-profile matches against Eddie Kingston, Claudio Castagnoli, and others, culminating in a successful submission victory over Vin Gerard at the season finale "Three-Fisted Tales".

Return to PAWF and Face Turn (2010–2024)

Returning to PAWF in 2010, Doug underwent a significant character evolution, turning face and becoming a crowd favorite. Embracing an anti-hero persona while retaining occasional heel nuances, he contributed to nurturing up-and-coming talent until his retirement.

Retirement (2024)

Doug announced his intention to retire exactly 30 years after his debut, on November 17, 2024. His retirement match at the St. Clair Shores Civic Center in St. Clair, Michigan, was an emotional showdown against longtime rival Jack Gallahay. Highlights included a daring Frog Splash through a table and a Devious Death Drop through the announcer table, culminating in a submission victory via the Scorpion Cross Lock. The post-match celebration featured an emotional embrace between the two rivals, marking a poignant conclusion to his storied career.

Professional Wrestling Style and Persona

Doug Devious is renowned for his versatile and innovative wrestling style. He combines technical wrestling, judo submission grappling, MMA-style strikes, and high-flying offense to deliver compelling performances across multiple promotions worldwide.

Signature Moves:

  • Kudo Driver – Back-to-back double underhook piledriver
  • D-5 – Fireman's carry facebuster
  • 5★ Frog Splash – Top-rope splash

Finishers:

  • Devious Death Drop (DDD) – Vertical suplex piledriver
  • Scorpion Cross Lock – Inverted sharpshooter combined with a double chickenwing
  • Diving Elbow Drop – Top-rope elbow drop
